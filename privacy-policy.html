Privacy Policy

The way Narayana Shikshayatan uses and safeguards any information you provide when using this website is outlined in this privacy statement. We're dedicated to making sure your privacy is safe. This privacy policy applies to all the data that is gathered about you.

Data We Gather:

Contact Details: Your name, email address, phone number, street, city, state, pin code, country, and IP address may be collected.

Data You Share: We gather data you provide publicly on our website or one of our third-party social media platforms.

Demographic Data: During your usage of our website, you may provide us with additional information, including your demographic data. We might collect this as a part of a survey too.

Additional Data: If you visit our website, we might gather data about your IP address and browser. We may check the website you came from, the amount of time you spent on our site, the pages you visited, or the website you visited after you left. We may also get information on the kind of mobile device you are using or the operating system version that is installed on your computer or device.

We Gather Information in Many Ways: When you complete any online application or request/inquiry form that we provide on our website or social media accounts, we get information straight from you. Additionally, we gather data if you contact us by phone or email with a question or leave a comment on one of our third-party social media accounts.

We Passively Get Information From You: We gather data about how you use our website using tracking techniques like Google Analytics, browser cookies, and other safe tools.

We Obtain Information About You from Third Parties: We might obtain information about you from the social media platform of a third party. Your name, email address, and phone number may be among them.

Utilisation of Your Private Data

1. We contact you for marketing purposes using the information you give us.

2. To answer your enquiries or requests, we use information. If you have applied online for any of our courses, we may use your information to verify your application.

3. Information is used to enhance our admissions services. Your information may be used by us to personalise your interaction with us. This can entail showing content according to your preferences.

4. We use data to examine visitor preferences and site trends. Your information might be used to improve our services and website. The information we get about you from third parties may be combined with the information we get from you.

5. Information is used for security. Information may be used to safeguard our websites, human resources, or organisation.

6. Information is used by us for marketing.

7. To send you transactional communications, we use information. We may send you pertinent WhatsApp messages, SMS messages, or emails.

8. We utilise data in ways that are otherwise allowed by law.

Information Exchange with Third Parties

1. Third parties who provide services on our behalf will receive information from us. We will communicate with our business associates.

2. If we believe that sharing information is necessary to protect ourselves or to comply with the law, we may do so. To respond to a court order or to meet any legal obligations, we shall exchange information. When looking into possible fraud, we might also provide it upon request from a government agency or investigative body.

Cookies

1. A cookie is a tiny file that requests authorisation to be stored on the hard disc of your computer. After you consent, the file is added, and the cookie notifies you when you visit a specific website or aids in web traffic analysis. Web apps can react to you personally, thanks to cookies. By collecting and storing information about your choices, the web application can adjust its functionality to your needs, preferences, and likes. Cookies from traffic logs are utilised to determine which pages are being viewed. This aids in the analysis of website traffic data and the enhancement of our website to better meet the demands of our patients. Only statistical analysis is performed on this data before it is deleted from the system.

2. Overall, by allowing us to track which pages you find helpful and which you do not, cookies help us give you a better website. Apart from the information you choose to share with us, cookies don't give us access to your computer or any other personal information. Cookies are something you can accept or reject. The majority of web browsers accept cookies by default; however, if you would like, you can often change your browser's settings to reject cookie use. This can keep you from using the website to its best potential.
